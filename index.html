About
Hello! I am a Master’s student in Transportation and Highway Engineering at Louisiana State University (LSU), where I serve as a Graduate Research Assistant on an LTRC-funded project focused on evaluating the noise and vibration impact of longitudinal rumble strips.

My research involves field testing, experimental design, and acoustic/vibration analysis based on AASHTO and SAE standards. I was awarded 2nd place at LSU’s CEE Graduate Student Research Conference (GSRC) for my work.

I earned my B.Sc. in Civil Engineering from Jordan University of Science and Technology (JUST), where I ranked 2nd out of 126 students. My undergraduate capstone project applied machine learning to predict crash severity and conduct traffic accident hotspot analysis.

I also completed a paid summer internship in Austria, working on laser scanning, GPS surveying, and developing 2D/3D mapping plans.

Outside of academics, I serve as the President of the ITE LSU Student Chapter.
