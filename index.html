About
Hello! I am a Master’s student in Transportation and Highway Engineering at Louisiana State University (LSU), where I serve as a Graduate Research Assistant on an LTRC-funded project investigating the noise and vibration impact of rumble strips.

My work involves field testing, experimental design, site selection, and acoustic/vibration analysis using AASHTO and SAE standards. I’ve presented multiple posters at the TRB 104th Annual Meeting and won 2nd place at LSU’s CEE Graduate Student Research Conference.

I hold a B.Sc. in Civil Engineering from Jordan University of Science and Technology (JUST), graduating 2nd out of 126 students, where I built a machine learning model to predict traffic crash severity.

I’m passionate about applying engineering and data science to improve roadway safety. Outside of academics, I lead the LSU ITE Student Chapter and engage in entrepreneurship, mentorship, and public speaking.
