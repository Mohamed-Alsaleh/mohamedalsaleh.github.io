About
Hello! I am a Master’s student in Transportation and Highway Engineering at Louisiana State University (LSU), where I serve as a Graduate Research Assistant on an LTRC-funded project focused on evaluating the noise and vibration impact of longitudinal rumble strips.

My work involves experimental design, site selection, field testing, and acoustic/vibration analysis using AASHTO and SAE standards. I’ve presented at the TRB 104th Annual Meeting and won 2nd place in the Graduate Student Research Conference (GSRC) at LSU.

I hold a B.Sc. in Civil Engineering from Jordan University of Science and Technology (JUST), where I ranked 2nd out of 126 students. My graduation project involved using machine learning to predict crash severity and performing hotspot analysis of traffic accidents.

I also completed a competitive, paid summer internship in Austria, working on laser scanning, GPS surveying, and 3D/2D mapping.

Outside academics, I serve as the President of the ITE LSU Student Chapter.
